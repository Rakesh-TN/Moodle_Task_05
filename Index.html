Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal was commissioned by Shah Jahan in 1631, to be built in the memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died on 17 June that year, while giving birth to their 14th child, Gauhara Begum.

India Gate

TThe India Gate was part of the work of the Imperial War Graves Commission (IWGC), which came into existence in December 1918 under the British rule for building war graves and memorials to soldiers who were killed in the First World War.

National Emblem of India

The National Emblem of India is an official seal used by the Central government, state government, and many government agencies as a representative emblem of the Union of India. It is adapted from the Lion Capital of Ashoka at Sarnath, Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh.
